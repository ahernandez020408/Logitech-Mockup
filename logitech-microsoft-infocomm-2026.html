Campaign Rationale
Why This. Why Now.

Microsoft 365 Copilot has crossed a threshold that most enterprise software never reaches in its first two years. At 420 million monthly active users in Q1 2026, up from 230 million just twelve months prior, Copilot is no longer a pilot program for forward-thinking IT teams. It is a standard-issue productivity layer at 64% of Fortune 500 companies, and enterprise license volumes have nearly tripled since early 2025. That kind of adoption curve does not slow down; it creates downstream demand. IT decision-makers who have already bought into the Copilot ecosystem are now asking a natural next question: what hardware do I need to make this actually work in a conference room?

That is the opening Logitech is uniquely positioned to fill. InfoComm 2026 is not a consumer electronics show; it is where AV integrators and enterprise buyers finalize hardware decisions for the next 12 months. The attendees walking the floor in Las Vegas are not curious early adopters. They are procurement leads with budget authority and a mandate to build rooms that support AI-powered workflows. When a Logitech Alliance Manager stands at booth C7050 and walks a prospect through Rally Camera AI with RightSight 2 auto-framing alongside a live Microsoft Teams Rooms and Microsoft 365 Copilot demo, it collapses the gap between software promise and physical reality. The camera sees the room. The room understands the meeting. Copilot surfaces what matters afterward.

The timing is intentional. Logitech's video collaboration segment contributed meaningfully to Q4 2026 revenue of $1.09 billion, and the strategic bet behind that number is that enterprises do not buy AI software and then figure out the hardware separately. They buy systems. This campaign is an integrated go-to-market program that spans paid and organic social, partner channels, email, and live event activation; it is the kind of cross-functional, ecosystem-wide initiative that defines how the Logitech Alliance Marketing team drives strategic growth through its Microsoft partnership. Executing it well requires tight operational coordination across teams, consistent asset management, and clear performance tracking from launch through post-event follow-up. That is the operational reality behind every campaign that looks effortless on the outside.

The scope of that ecosystem role becomes clearer when you look at the full InfoComm session calendar. On June 17 alone, Alliance Manager Michele Perini is a featured speaker across three separate partner sessions spanning Microsoft, Google, and Zoom. No other hardware vendor shows up that way. It is a direct signal of what makes the Logitech Alliance Marketing team distinctive: rather than building siloed campaigns for each partner, the team drives thought leadership and ecosystem activations that reinforce Logitech's platform-agnostic leadership across the entire UC landscape. The Microsoft campaign benefits from that credibility, and it contributes to it.

Key Messages
What We Are Saying
Campaign Headline
Copilot is smarter when it can see the room. Logitech makes that possible.
Proof Point 01

The Digital Cocoon. AI sees and hears the room completely.

RightSight 2 and RightSound 2 work in concert to create what Logitech calls the "Digital Cocoon": an immersive, high-fidelity input environment that gives Microsoft 365 Copilot exactly the visual and audio data it needs to transcribe, summarize, and act. Rally Camera AI Pro extends that experience to town hall spaces, large boardrooms, and training facilities where single-camera setups fall short.

Proof Point 02

Copilot catches every voice. Zone Wireless 2 makes sure of it.

Zone Wireless 2 combines hybrid active noise cancellation with AI-powered microphones to isolate speech even in open, noisy workspaces. IT teams manage deployments at scale through Logitech Sync, the same platform used across the certified Teams Rooms portfolio. Clear audio is not a nice-to-have; it is what Copilot transcription and summarization depend on.

Proof Point 03

Certified hardware. Native Windows 11 integration. Zero friction deployment.

Logitech's Microsoft Teams Rooms certified portfolio integrates natively with the Microsoft stack. MX Master 4 for Business takes that integration further as the first productivity mouse with native Windows 11 haptic feedback, a detail that anchors the full Logitech x Microsoft story from the room to the desk. IT teams get consistent remote monitoring via Logitech Sync and a roadmap built in lockstep with Microsoft.
